Chris AI Agent — Why Credits Make Sense for AI (But Not for Humans)

Today, Chris charges 150 credits per 3-minute call (50 credits/minute). That's roughly $0.50/minute — 10x the cost of a human call. This 10x multiplier reflects the compute cost of STT + LLM reasoning + TTS.

This is the strongest argument for eventually having credits for AI agent calls. But it's also evidence that human calls and AI calls are fundamentally different cost structures that shouldn't be forced into the same billing unit prematurely.

No alpha usage data to calibrate. Too small = frustration. Too large = margin loss. Must use alpha data before locking GA tiers.

4
Inbound call pricing gap. Fast-Follow

Paper Tigers (Overblown Concerns)

1
"SMBs can't handle usage-based pricing"
SMBs handle usage-based pricing daily (phone plans, AWS, Mailchimp). The issue is abstraction (credits), not usage.
2
"We'll be locked into buckets forever"
Salesforce added Flex Credits on top of existing minute buckets without disrupting the base. Migration paths exist.

Elephants (Unspoken Worries — Need Investigation)

1
Can billing infrastructure handle real-time telephony credit deduction?
Call minutes are continuous, not discrete events. Needs eng feasibility assessment before committing to credits.
2
Is the "credits economy" direction a suggestion or a mandate?
If leadership has pre-decided, this research is academic. Need clarity on decision authority before proceeding.
3
BYON / number porting impact on pricing.
If users port existing numbers later, does the $/number/month model change? Needs definition before V3.

Pre-Mortem: What If Credits Fail?

It's January 2027. monday Smart Call monetization has failed. What happened?

monday launched with unified AI credits at GA. Initial uptake was strong from free alpha accounts. Within 60 days, three problems emerged:

1. Sales cycles lengthened 20%. Prospects couldn't understand credits vs. HubSpot's "2,000 minutes." AEs spent time educating instead of closing.

2. Support was overwhelmed. "Credit math confusion" became a top ticket category. Users who ran out of credits assumed calling was broken.

3. CFOs refused to renew. Two mid-market accounts switched back to Aircall: "At least I know what $40/user/month means."

The team pivoted to buckets in Q4 2026, but Smart Call's reputation as "confusing" had spread. Re-engagement rates for lapsed users: under 15%.

Decision Framework

Five Key Questions

#QuestionAnswer (Based on Research)
1 What % of usage will be AI-agent vs. human calls in 12 months? <20%. Chris AI voice agent is 2027+ roadmap. Credits for telephony are premature.
2 Who is the buyer? 86% SMB. They think in minutes. They compare to Aircall and phone plans.
3 Does this solve a customer problem or an internal problem? Buckets = customer problem (predictable costs). Credits = internal problem (platform consistency).
4 What's the migration path? Salesforce blueprint: Launch on buckets, add credits for AI agents when they ship. Two systems in parallel.
5 Can we offer both? Yes. Option C does this. Salesforce validates the approach.

The Forced Tradeoff

"If you choose credits now, you're deprioritizing sales velocity — AEs will spend time educating instead of closing. If you choose buckets now, you're deprioritizing platform consistency — Smart Call will be the odd one out in the credits economy. What's the higher cost?"

The data suggests sales velocity is the higher cost. Smart Call's #1 hypothesis is behavior change — getting reps to switch from personal phones. Adding pricing confusion compounds adoption friction. Platform consistency can be achieved later. Lost sales velocity in the launch window cannot be recovered.

Recommended De-Risk Experiment

During alpha: Run a pricing perception concept test with alpha accounts.

Show each account champion two presentations — one bucket-based, one credit-based. Ask three questions:
1. "Can you explain this pricing to your CFO?"
2. "Can you predict your monthly spend?"
3. "Which would you choose for your team?"

Kill criteria: If >60% can explain buckets but <40% can explain credits, the market has spoken. This test costs nothing and takes one week.

Why 6 Credits per Minute?

Rate$/MinProsCons
5 credits $0.05 Round number, lowest customer price Underprices 500-min tier ($25 vs planned $30), margin thins to ~28% on full COGS
6 credits $0.06 Matches planned 500-min tier exactly ($30), 58% margin on PSTN COGS Eliminates volume discount taper at higher tiers

Human vs. AI positioning: At 6 credits/min (human) vs. 50 credits/min (Chris AI), the 8x multiplier creates an intuitive price signal — AI autonomy is premium, human calls are commodity.

FAQ — What Sales Will Be Asked

Modeled on real questions from #ask-monday-notetaker — every pattern below has an analog from the Notetaker launch.

QuestionAnswer
"How much does a call actually cost?" 6 credits per minute = $0.06. A typical 3-minute sales call costs 18 credits ($0.18). Everything is included — recording, transcript, AI summary.
"Is the AI transcription extra?" Included. 6 credits/min covers EVERYTHING. No separate AI action charge. This is different from workflows where AI blocks use credits — Smart Call bundles it all in.
"How do credits relate to minutes?" Divide credits by 6 to get minutes. An 8,000-credit bucket ≈ 1,300 minutes. A 20,000 bucket ≈ 3,000 minutes.
"What if they run out mid-call?" Calls in progress are NEVER cut off. The call completes, and the account goes into an overage buffer. Alerts fire at 50%, 80%, 100%.
"Customer uses calling AND Notetaker AND workflows. How to budget?" All from the same credit pool. If they need 20K for calling and 5K for AI usage, recommend the 40K bucket. Use the calculator to size it.
"Per user or per account?" Credits are pooled at the account level. The whole team shares the credit bucket. Per-user consumption is tracked in the admin dashboard.
"Can admins control who can call?" Yes. Admin controls available from day one. Grant/revoke Smart Call access per user from Settings → Usage.

Objection Handling

Prospect SaysRespond With
"HubSpot just gives me 2,000 minutes." HubSpot charges $100/user/month — $1,000/month for 10 reps. Our 80,000 credit bucket covers 10 reps calling an hour a day for $800/month, AND those credits work for AI features too. Plus we include transcription and AI summaries that HubSpot charges extra for.
"I don't understand credits." Think of it this way: 6 credits = 1 minute. Your 20,000 credit bucket = ~3,000 minutes. If you call less one month, those credits aren't wasted — they power your AI workflows instead.
"Aircall is $30/user and I don't have to think." Aircall × 10 reps = $300/month plus integration fees. Smart Call is native — every call auto-logs with AI summaries. 80,000 credits covers 10 heavy-calling reps for $800/month with no integration, no separate recording tool, no manual data entry.
"What if we call more than expected?" Auto-recharge is available — set a threshold and we add credits automatically. Or upgrade buckets mid-cycle. Active calls are never interrupted.
"How do I explain this to my CFO?" [reps] × [calls/day] × [avg duration] × 6 credits × 22 days = monthly credits. Pick the bucket. Monthly cost is fixed and predictable. We also provide a usage dashboard to track actuals vs. budget.

6. Critical Considerations

6A. The Shared Pool Question

Smart Call credits draw from the same AI credit pool as Notetaker, workflows, AI blocks, and Sidekick.

Upside

  • Flexibility: unused calling credits power AI workflows
  • One balance, one dashboard, one invoice line
  • Cross-sell: customers discover AI features through credit headroom

Risk

  • Budget competition: heavy calling starves AI workflows
  • Notetaker precedent: "free" summary costs credits in workflows
  • Attribution confusion: what consumed my credits?
Recommendation: Make the shared pool work through radical transparency. The admin dashboard MUST break down consumption by source (Smart Call vs. Notetaker vs. Workflows vs. Other). Smart Call's 6 credits/min rate NEVER triggers additional charges — even when the AI summary feeds downstream workflows.

6B. Volume Discount Gap

The planned dollar pricing had a taper ($0.06 → $0.055 → $0.05/min). Uniform 6 credits/min eliminates it.

OptionMechanismProsCons
Accept uniform 6 credits/min at all volumes Simplest. One rate. High-volume pays 20% more
Smart Call Packs Bundled credit blocks at a discount Feels like a deal Adds SKU complexity
Bucket discounts Larger buckets = lower $/credit Applies to ALL features Changes $0.01/credit assumption
Recommended: Start uniform at GA. Monitor alpha feedback. If high-volume accounts push back, introduce bucket-level volume discounts (not telephony-specific packs).

7. Notetaker Lessons Applied

Source: Analysis of 80+ messages in #ask-monday-notetaker. Every pattern below has direct evidence from the Notetaker launch and a specific fix for Smart Call.
1
Credit cost per activity is undocumented
"The exact number of AI credits consumed per hour of recording is not specified in the provided documents." — Jose Escobar, Knowledge Expert team
Fix: Publish "6 credits/minute" in help center, internal wiki, and sales materials before launch. Pin it in #ask-smart-call.
2
No per-user usage visibility
"Is there a way to see on BigBrain the AI Notetaker consumption (hours taken and by whom)?" — Angelica Balbuena
Fix: Admin → Usage shows per-rep credit consumption: total credits, calling minutes, calls made, trend chart. Available from day one.
3
Admin controls arrived late
"How can I ensure that only admins use the notetaker?" — Hanna Grande. "Can customers select who can use the notetaker?" — Daniel Tsivin. "How does an Admin Disable Notetaker?" — Troy Gaub.
Fix: Admin controls from day one. Settings → Smart Call → User Access. Grant/revoke per user. Set per-user monthly credit limits.
4
Trial management is ad-hoc chaos
"Is there a way we can add additional notetaker trial hours?" — Shira Lechner. "Customer used all trial hours by leaving Zoom open." — Melissa Winters. "Is there anyway to do this for 1 day?" — Kristen Latrenta.
Fix: Defined trial process: X credits allocation per account. Sales Ops one-click extension. Max 1 extension. Auto-expiry after 30 days.
5
Accidental usage burns budget
"Customer used all of their Notetaker trial hours by leaving a Zoom open unoccupied." — Melissa Winters
Fix: Idle protection: auto-disconnect after 60s silence (15s warning tone). Max call duration cap (default 120 min). Pocket-dial detection (terminate if no voice in first 10s).
6
"Included" vs. "extra" is unclear
"The user asks if there is a way to pull the summary without using the 'summarize with AI block' to save on AI actions." — Ron Tribuchovski
Fix: Absolute clarity: "6 credits/minute includes the call, recording, transcription, AND the AI summary. Workflow AI actions have their own cost — but the call itself is always just 6 credits/minute."
